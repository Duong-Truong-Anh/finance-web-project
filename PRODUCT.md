# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Adults planning long-term financial independence. They use Flowstate in focused, deliberate sessions
— not reactive check-ins. They are comfortable with numbers and have goals spanning decades. They
want to understand where their money goes, how much flows into investments, and what that compounding
looks like at 10, 20, and 30 years. They do not want to be gamified, motivated, or surprised.

## Product Purpose

Flowstate is a personal cash flow management tool with a long-term, multi-asset investment simulator.
It has two phases: a contribution window (months 1-60) where the user enters income and expenses, and
a compounding window (months 61-360) where the portfolio compounds. Net cash flow allocates across
five asset classes per the assignment brief — stocks 50% (split equally across 5 user-selected
tickers), savings 10%, cash 10%, gold 10%, USD 10%. Stock growth is shown across three deterministic
scenarios (15% / 17.5% / 20% annualized); non-stock asset classes use single rates per their typical
long-term profile. Success means the user walks away with a believable, verifiable 30-year projection
— and a clear sense of how each asset class contributes — they can act on.

## Brand Personality

Clear, honest, unhurried.

The product does not celebrate wins, manufacture urgency, or show off. It presents what is true,
lets the user sit with it, and gets out of the way. The tone is that of a well-designed instrument:
quiet confidence, no noise.

## Anti-references

- **Retail fintech (Mint, YNAB, Copilot)**: consumer-warm pastels, motivational copy, gamification
  badges, friendly illustrations, progress streaks. Flowstate is not encouraging anyone.
- **Crypto dashboards**: neon on black, candlestick overload, ticker-tape anxiety, speculative
  energy. Flowstate is about patient compounding, not speculation.
- **Generic SaaS analytics**: hero-metric cards, gradient numbers, identical icon-heading-text
  tile grids, AI-dashboard clichés. Carbon components with discipline, not SaaS templates.
- **Investment-bank terminals (Bloomberg)**: monospace everything, information overload, zero
  breathing room, density that signals competence. Flowstate earns trust through clarity, not
  through overwhelming the user.

## Design Principles

1. **Numbers deserve trust.** Calculations are surfaced, not hidden. Projections show their
   assumptions. Precision comes before impressiveness.

2. **Long view over anxiety.** The product is about 30-year horizons. Design reinforces patience:
   no urgent alerts, no streaks, no loss aversion cues. The dominant emotion is calm.

3. **Density serves comprehension.** Show the right information at the right time. Not sparse for
   aesthetics, not maximal for power. Every element earns its space by adding understanding.

4. **IBM discipline, personal scale.** Carbon-native at IBM quality, applied to one person's life
   decisions. The discipline of the design system is the product's integrity signal.

5. **Transparency over persuasion.** No black-box projections. No copy that manages feelings. The
   interface informs; the user decides.
